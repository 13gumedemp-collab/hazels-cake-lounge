My story

From my mother's kitchen to yours.

Some of my earliest memories are flour dusted and sweet.

I grew up watching my mother bake. She never used a timer and she never measured anything twice. It was all feeling, instinct, and love pressed into every bowl she touched. I would drag a chair to the kitchen counter just to be close to it, close to her, close to whatever magic was happening.

She taught me that a cake is never just a cake. It is a moment.

It is someone's birthday sung out loud. It is a grandmother's recipe kept alive. It is the thing people remember long after the candles go out.

I started baking on my own as a young girl and I never really stopped. Then life took me in a different direction for a while. I joined Standard Bank, stepped into the corporate world, and threw myself into it. I was good at it too. But somewhere between the meetings and the spreadsheets, I kept thinking about my mother's kitchen. I kept thinking about cake.

After a couple of years, I made a decision that felt terrifying and absolutely right at the same time. I left. Because I realised that what I was meant to do, what I had always been meant to do, was this.

I did not just leap and hope for the best. I invested in my craft properly. I enrolled in professional baking courses, trained under people who had dedicated their lives to this art, and I came out the other side with both the passion I was born with and the formal expertise to back it up. What my mother gave me was heart. The training gave me precision. Together, they are everything I bring to every cake I make.

Today, when you order from me, you are not just getting something homemade and hopeful. You are getting years of lived experience, professional training, and a genuine love for what lands on your table.

Every order I take is personal to me. I want your cake to be the one people talk about long after the plates are cleared.

Welcome. I am so glad you found me.
